Air Quality - Northern Europe

The pollutants displayed are selected based on recommendations by the 2008/50/EC EU directive. Their impacts are as follows:

  • PM10 and PM2.5 are airborne particles with diameters of 10 and 2.5 micrometres respectively. Due to their small size, they can enter the body via inhalation and accumulate in internal organs.
  • SO2 (sulphur dioxide) is a byproduct of the combustion of sulphur-containing fuels, is a component of acid rain which raises the acidity level of soils, hurting agriculture, and land and aquatic systems.
  • CO (carbon monoxide) is a colourless and odourless gas, diminishes the human bodyâ€™s ability to absorb oxygen and in high concentrations, results in death
  • C6H6 (benzene) is carcinogenic and is released by using petroleum products containing it, as well as smoking
  • O3 (ground level ozone) is a product of reactions between oxides of nitrogen and volatile organic compounds, can inflame the respiratory system and oxidise other pollutants in the air to create smog.
